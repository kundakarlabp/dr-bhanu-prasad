# Evidence-First Medical Review

## Purpose

Produce a clinically usable synthesis from authoritative and current sources. The intended reader may be an infectious-disease specialist; do not dilute the answer into generic patient education unless requested.

## Workflow

1. Define the exact clinical question using population, syndrome/pathogen, intervention, comparator, outcome, and setting where applicable.
2. Search current sources whenever guidance, resistance, drug information, epidemiology, or standards may have changed.
3. Prioritize sources in this order:
   - current professional-society or public-health guidelines
   - primary clinical trials, cohorts, pharmacokinetic studies, and systematic reviews
   - high-quality specialty reviews for synthesis
   - official drug labels or interaction resources for pharmacology
4. Check publication date, population, infection site, immune status, geography, microbiology method, susceptibility standard, dose, duration, and outcome definition.
5. Reconcile disagreements explicitly. Do not average incompatible recommendations.
6. Translate evidence into a practical recommendation, including dose, route, duration, monitoring, interactions, renal/hepatic adjustment, and source-control implications when supported.
7. State where evidence is indirect, observational, in vitro, pediatric extrapolation, or expert opinion.

## Required output

- **Clinical bottom line**
- **Evidence synthesis**
- **Practical recommendation**
- **Monitoring and safety**
- **Important exceptions/uncertainty**
- **Citations**

Use tables only when they improve comparison of guidelines, regimens, doses, or evidence quality.

## Quality checks

- Every unstable factual claim has a source.
- No citation is fabricated or attached to a claim it does not support.
- Drug doses match age, weight, renal/hepatic function, indication, and route.
- In vitro activity is not presented as proven clinical efficacy.
- Breakpoints and susceptibility standards are identified when they affect interpretation.
- Geographic resistance data are not generalized without qualification.

## Failure and uncertainty handling

When evidence is absent or conflicting, say so directly. Give the most defensible approach, identify the assumptions, and specify what additional microbiology, imaging, pharmacokinetic, or clinical data would change the recommendation.
