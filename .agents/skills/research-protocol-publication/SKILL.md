# Research Protocol and Publication

## Purpose

Create internally consistent, submission-ready research material in the user's professional voice, with verified references and appropriate reporting standards.

## Workflow

1. Determine the deliverable, audience, institutional format, study design, clinical domain, and stage of work.
2. Build a requirements checklist from the supplied template, ethics comments, journal instructions, or grant call.
3. Define the research question, rationale, gap, objectives, hypotheses, population, exposure/intervention, comparator, outcomes, timepoints, and setting.
4. Align all downstream elements:
   - eligibility and recruitment
   - variables and operational definitions
   - sample-size assumptions
   - data collection and quality control
   - statistical analysis
   - missing-data handling
   - ethics, consent, privacy, and safety
   - dissemination and authorship
5. Select applicable standards such as CONSORT, STROBE, STARD, PRISMA, CARE, TRIPOD/TRIPOD+AI, SPIRIT, RECORD, or local IEC requirements.
6. Search and verify references. Use primary or authoritative sources and the requested citation style.
7. Draft in the user's requested tone and structure. Avoid inflated claims, generic filler, fabricated novelty, and repetitive AI phrasing.
8. Perform a consistency audit before delivery.

## Consistency audit

Confirm that:

- title, rationale, objectives, outcomes, variables, CRF, and analysis describe the same study
- primary outcome drives sample size and statistical plan
- timepoints are identical across protocol, CRF, consent, and analysis
- inclusion/exclusion criteria are feasible and ethically justified
- all planned analyses map to collected variables
- questionnaire or score validation claims are accurate
- ethics documents use understandable language and do not overpromise benefit
- references exist and bibliographic fields are correct

## Required output

Adapt to the task, but normally provide:

- submission-ready text
- concise list of unresolved decisions
- reporting-guideline or IEC compliance table when useful
- verified reference list
- explicit description of any assumptions made

## Artifact handling

When the user requests DOCX, PDF, spreadsheet, or slides, invoke the artifact-production workflow and deliver the actual file after rendering/validation.

## Failure and uncertainty handling

Do not invent unavailable results, recruitment numbers, validation statistics, effect sizes, approvals, registrations, or references. Mark placeholders clearly and state exactly what information is required to finalize them.
